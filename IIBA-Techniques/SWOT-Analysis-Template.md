# SWOT Analysis Technique
*IIBA Technique: SWOT Analysis*

## Overview
SWOT Analysis is a strategic planning technique used to identify Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats related to business competition or project planning.

## SWOT Matrix Template

### Strengths (Internal Factors)
*Positive attributes that are within control*
- [ ] **Resource Strengths**: Skilled team, adequate funding, technology assets
- [ ] **Capability Strengths**: Efficient processes, strong brand, customer loyalty
- [ ] **Competitive Advantages**: Unique features, patents, market position
- [ ] **Strategic Strengths**: Leadership, innovation culture, partnerships

### Weaknesses (Internal Factors)
*Negative attributes that are within control*
- [ ] **Resource Limitations**: Budget constraints, staffing gaps, outdated technology
- [ ] **Capability Gaps**: Inefficient processes, skill shortages, quality issues
- [ ] **Competitive Disadvantages**: Higher costs, limited features, poor reputation
- [ ] **Strategic Weaknesses**: Lack of direction, resistance to change, poor morale

### Opportunities (External Factors)
*External factors that could be exploited to advantage*
- [ ] **Market Opportunities**: New customer segments, emerging markets, trends
- [ ] **Technology Opportunities**: New platforms, tools, automation potential
- [ ] **Strategic Opportunities**: Partnerships, acquisitions, diversification
- [ ] **Regulatory Opportunities**: Policy changes, incentives, standards

### Threats (External Factors)
*External factors that could cause trouble*
- [ ] **Market Threats**: New competitors, changing customer preferences
- [ ] **Technology Threats**: Disruptive technologies, obsolescence
- [ ] **Strategic Threats**: Economic downturns, supply chain issues
- [ ] **Regulatory Threats**: New regulations, compliance requirements

## Implementation Guide

### Preparation Phase
1. **Define Scope**: Clearly bound what you're analyzing (project, product, organization)
2. **Gather Data**: Collect relevant internal and external information
3. **Assemble Team**: Include diverse perspectives (business, technical, customer)

### Workshop Facilitation
1. **Individual Brainstorming** (15 minutes): Silent generation of SWOT items
2. **Group Sharing** (30 minutes): Collect and cluster similar ideas
3. **Prioritization** (20 minutes): Vote on most important items in each category
4. **Action Planning** (25 minutes): Develop strategies for key items

### Analysis Framework
**SO Strategies** (Strengths-Opportunities)
- Leverage strengths to capitalize on opportunities
- *Example: Use strong brand reputation to enter new markets*

**WO Strategies** (Weaknesses-Opportunities)
- Overcome weaknesses by taking advantage of opportunities
- *Example: Address skill gaps through training to support new technology*

**ST Strategies** (Strengths-Threats)
- Use strengths to reduce vulnerability to threats
- *Example: Leverage financial strength to withstand economic downturns*

**WT Strategies** (Weaknesses-Threats)
- Defensive actions to prevent weaknesses making you susceptible to threats
- *Example: Implement cost controls to mitigate budget constraints*

## Example: Software Project SWOT

### Strengths
- Experienced development team
- Modern technology stack
- Strong executive sponsorship

### Weaknesses
- Limited documentation
- Tight project timeline
- Dependencies on external teams

### Opportunities
- Growing market demand
- New integration possibilities
- Potential for industry leadership

### Threats
- Emerging competitors
- Changing technology standards
- Economic uncertainty

## Output & Next Steps
- **Strategic Actions**: Convert SWOT analysis into actionable initiatives
- **Monitoring Plan**: Track changes in SWOT factors over time
- **Communication**: Share results with relevant stakeholders
